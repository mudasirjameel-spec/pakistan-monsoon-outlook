Shawwal Moon Update 2026

Crescent visibility parameters for Eid-ul-Fitr 1447 AH.

According to astronomical models, the birth of the new moon will occur on March 19 at 6:23 AM.

On the evening of March 19 at sunset, the moon's age will be only 12 hours and 38 minutes, with an illumination of just 0.4%, and it will remain on the horizon for barely 26 minutes. Due to these parameters, there is no chance of the moon being sighted in any part of Pakistan on that evening.

However, on the evening of March 20, the moon's age will be approximately 37 hours and 38 minutes, and it will remain on the horizon for 1 hour and 29 minutes after sunset, making it extremely easy to sight without any optical aids.

Although the moon will be easily visible across all of Pakistan on March 20, the most favorable astronomical positions for observation will be coastal Sindh and southwestern Balochistan (especially Gwadar, Pasni, Jiwani, and Karachi).

Pakistan Monsoon Outlook 2026

Mudasir Jameel· Climate Analysis· 2026

The 2026 southwest monsoon season presents a cautiously pessimistic signal for southern Pakistan, driven by a developing El Niño pattern in the central Pacific Ocean. Northern regions maintain a more uncertain but relatively better-supported outlook owing to monsoon trough interactions and orographic dynamics.

Pacific Ocean Signal

Sea surface temperature anomalies in the Niño 3.4 region have trended positive through early 2026, crossing the +0.5 °C threshold that defines El Niño onset. During established El Niño episodes, the Walker Circulation weakens — reducing Arabian Sea moisture convergence and suppressing the cross-equatorial Somali Jet, both critical mechanisms for delivering southwest monsoon rainfall into Pakistan.

Multi-model ensemble guidance from ECMWF, NCEP and BOM projects this warm Pacific anomaly persisting through June–September. The magnitude remains moderate rather than strong, which limits the degree of expected suppression.

Arabian Sea Conditions

Despite the unfavourable Pacific background, the North Arabian Sea continues to hold above-normal sea surface temperatures. This residual warmth provides a secondary moisture source that can sustain occasional inland moisture surges and support convective activity near the coast. Karachi and coastal Sindh may experience episodic heavy rain events even within a below-normal season.

Monsoon Trough and Northern Incursions

The monsoon trough is expected to establish itself over the Indo-Gangetic Plain during July. During active phases, this trough shifts northward, allowing Bay of Bengal moisture systems to track westward into Punjab and the upper Indus Valley. This mechanism remains viable under El Niño conditions, though active spells will be shorter and less frequent than neutral or La Niña years.

Regional Outlook

Sindh Below Normal

El Niño suppression most pronounced here. Seasonal deficit of 25–40% below LPA is the most likely outcome. Isolated intense events from Arabian Sea surges remain possible.

Southern Balochistan Below Normal

Limited monsoon penetration expected. El Niño reduces both southwest monsoon incursions and pre-monsoon systems. Coastal Makran may see marginal benefit.

Punjab Near Normal

Bay of Bengal disturbances hold stronger influence in the monsoon belt. Active BoB lows can deliver substantial rainfall even during El Niño. Above-normal possible in northeast.

Khyber Pakhtunkhwa Near–Above Normal

Benefits from orographic lifting along Hindu Kush foothills and monsoon–westerly interactions in late season. Cautiously optimistic outlook relative to the national picture.

Northern Areas Near Normal

Gilgit-Baltistan and upper KP are less ENSO-sensitive than southern Pakistan. GLOF risk may be moderated given reduced snowmelt. Near-normal outcome anticipated.

Climate Drivers

Five large-scale ocean and atmosphere systems govern each monsoon season. Understanding their current state is the foundation of any seasonal forecast.

01
Developing El Niño · 2026

ENSO — El Niño Southern Oscillation

ENSO is the dominant interannual climate signal for South Asian monsoon variability. Warm Pacific anomalies (El Niño) weaken the Walker Circulation, reduce moisture transport into the Arabian Sea, and suppress monsoon convection over southern Pakistan. For 2026, a moderate El Niño is the leading scenario, with Niño 3.4 indices above +0.5 °C — historically associated with below-normal rainfall in Sindh and coastal Balochistan.

📊

Moderate El Niño in 2026 creates the most unfavourable Pacific forcing for Pakistan's monsoon since 2015. Suppression will be strongest across Sindh and the lower Indus basin.

Indian Ocean Dipole (IOD)

The IOD measures the sea surface temperature difference between the western and eastern Indian Ocean. A positive IOD warms the western basin, strengthening moisture gradient toward South Asia. A negative IOD has the opposite effect. For 2026, IOD is currently neutral but trending toward negative conditions — which would compound El Niño suppression for southern Pakistan.

🔬

Historical analogue years where both El Niño and negative IOD co-occurred (2015, 2009) are associated with notable monsoon deficits across southern Pakistan.

03
Arabian Sea Warm · BoB Near-Normal

Sea Surface Temperature Anomalies

SSTs in the Arabian Sea and Bay of Bengal directly modulate available monsoon moisture. The North Arabian Sea remains anomalously warm in early 2026 (+0.4–0.7 °C above normal), providing a compensating moisture source even as Pacific El Niño suppresses the main circulation. Bay of Bengal SSTs are near normal, supporting moderate low-pressure genesis. Somali Jet strength is expected slightly below normal due to weaker temperature gradients.

🌊

Warm North Arabian Sea in 2026 is the key mitigating factor. It limits how severe the El Niño-driven deficit can become, particularly for coastal Sindh and southern Punjab.

04
Below-Normal Snow · Strong Heating

Tibetan Plateau Heating & Snow Cover

The Tibetan Plateau acts as a high-altitude heat source in summer — warm air rising over Tibet drives upper-level divergence that positions the South Asian anticyclone and steers BoB lows westward into Pakistan. Below-normal HKH snow cover in early 2026 accelerates spring heating, supporting an earlier monsoon onset and stronger upper-level dynamics. This partially counteracts El Niño suppression for northern Pakistan.

🏔

Below-normal snow cover accelerates GLOF risk in Gilgit-Baltistan. Warmer-than-normal glacial conditions through the summer are anticipated.

05
Active Monitoring Required

Madden–Julian Oscillation (MJO)

The MJO is a 30–60 day tropical wave of enhanced convection propagating eastward around the globe. When its active phase is positioned over the Indian Ocean during monsoon months, it can temporarily overwhelm El Niño suppression and deliver intense rainfall pulses across Pakistan. Even in El Niño years, 1–2 significant MJO events typically produce active monsoon periods. MJO forecasts beyond 2–3 weeks are unreliable and must be monitored in real time.

🌀

MJO is the key wild-card for 2026. A well-timed MJO event over the Indian Ocean in July or August could dramatically alter the character of individual months within the season.
