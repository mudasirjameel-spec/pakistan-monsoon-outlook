Upcoming Western Disturbances

Significant Rainfall Accumulation Expected (March 18 onwards)

A series of potent Western Disturbances are expected to advance toward Pakistan beginning March 18, initiating a prolonged spell of widespread precipitation that is likely to persist through the latter part of the month. These systems are projected to influence nearly every province, delivering significant rainfall across much of the country.

Khyber Pakhtunkhwa and the northern mountainous regions are expected to bear the brunt of this activity, where cumulative precipitation may exceed 200 mm, accompanied by periods of intense downpours. Central and western belts will also experience substantial rainfall, with anticipated totals ranging between 60-150 mm around Quetta and parts of southern Balochistan, including Gwadar, Pishukan, Panjgur, and Turbat. Across Punjab, rainfall accumulations are projected to fall within the 80-120 mm range.

Even the southern coastal belt is likely to benefit from this active pattern. Areas around Karachi and the broader Sindh littoral could receive 30-50 mm of rainfall.

🌧️
Overview: Overall, this sequence of Western Disturbances appears poised to produce a well-distributed and significant rainfall episode across Pakistan, marking one of the more consequential wet spells of the season.
Astronomical Data

Shawwal Moon Update 2026

Crescent visibility parameters for Eid-ul-Fitr 1447 AH.

According to astronomical models, the birth of the new moon will occur on March 19 at 6:23 AM PKT.

On the evening of March 19 at sunset, the moon's age will be only 12 hours and 38 minutes, with an illumination of just 0.4%, and it will remain on the horizon for barely 26 minutes. Due to these extremely weak parameters, there is absolutely no chance of the moon being sighted in any part of Pakistan on that evening.

However, on the following evening of March 20, the moon's age will be approximately 37 hours and 38 minutes. It will remain on the horizon for 1 hour and 29 minutes after sunset, making it extremely easy to sight without any optical aids.

Although the moon will be easily visible across all of Pakistan on March 20, the most favorable astronomical positions for observation will be coastal Sindh and southwestern Balochistan (especially Gwadar, Pasni, Jiwani, and Karachi).

Pakistan Monsoon Outlook 2026

Analysis by Mudasir Jameel· Meteorological Research· 2026

Based on the latest models, the 2026 monsoon looks highly suppressed for the southern half of Pakistan. However, shifted monsoon trough dynamics will likely push excess moisture northward, favoring the upper regions.

Pacific Ocean Signal

Looking at the latest NINO3.4 SST anomaly plumes, temperatures are climbing steeply. Forecasts project anomalies pushing well past the +1.5°C threshold by July and August. This means a strong El Niño is actively forming. For Pakistan, a strong El Niño historically weakens the Walker Circulation, which severely reduces Arabian Sea moisture convergence and suppresses the cross-equatorial Somali Jet over Sindh and Balochistan.

Precipitation Outlook Breakdown

According to the latest probability models, the southern half of Pakistan (Sindh and Balochistan) is dominated by dry signals. However, during El Niño years, the monsoon trough frequently shifts northward toward the Himalayan foothills.

This shift, combined with mid-latitude westerly interactions, historically drives above-normal rainfall across Punjab, Khyber Pakhtunkhwa, and Gilgit-Baltistan. These northern regions should remain on high alert for intense downpours and glacial melt flooding.

*Please note: These are early spring observations. Climate models can fluctuate, and I will continue to update this forecast as the core season approaches.

Regional Outlook

Sindh Below Normal

El Niño suppression will hit hardest here. A severe deficit is the leading scenario. Isolated intense events from Arabian Sea surges remain the only major rainfall hope.

S. Balochistan Below Normal

Monsoon penetration will be severely limited. The strong El Niño pattern sharply reduces both southwest monsoon incursions and late-summer systems.

Punjab Above Normal

Benefiting from shifted monsoon trough dynamics during El Niño, Punjab is expected to receive above-normal rainfall, particularly in the northern and central belts.

KPK Above Normal

Orographic lifting combined with increased westerly interactions will likely drive above-normal precipitation. High risk of flash flooding in mountainous terrains.

Rapidly Warming · 2026

ENSO — El Niño Southern Oscillation

ENSO is the dominant interannual climate signal for South Asian monsoon variability. Pacific anomalies are shooting upward, heavily favoring a strong El Niño. This weakens the Walker Circulation and suppresses monsoon convection over southern Pakistan.

📊

Model plumes project anomalies crossing +1.5°C to +2.5°C by peak summer. This kind of Pacific forcing historically severely limits rainfall in Sindh and coastal Balochistan.

Indian Ocean Dipole (IOD)

The IOD measures the sea surface temperature difference across the Indian Ocean. A positive IOD warms the western basin, pulling moisture toward Pakistan. A negative IOD does the opposite. Currently, the IOD is neutral but models lean toward negative conditions, which would compound the El Niño dry spell in the south.

🔬

If a strong El Niño pairs with a negative IOD, we are looking at an analogue similar to 2015, which brought notable monsoon deficits across the lower half of the country.

SSTs in the Arabian Sea and Bay of Bengal directly modulate available monsoon moisture. The North Arabian Sea remains anomalously warm, which is the only major mitigating factor right now against the Pacific El Niño. This residual heat can sustain localized moisture surges.

🌊

This warmth means that while overall rain will likely be below normal for Sindh, when it does rain along the coast, it can be short and explosive.

Below-Normal Snow · Strong Heating

Tibetan Plateau Heating & Snow Cover

The Tibetan Plateau acts as a high-altitude heat source in summer — warm air rising over Tibet drives upper-level divergence that positions the South Asian anticyclone and steers BoB lows westward into Pakistan. Below-normal HKH snow cover in early 2026 accelerates spring heating, supporting an earlier monsoon onset for northern Pakistan.

🏔

Below-normal snow cover accelerates GLOF risk in Gilgit-Baltistan. Warmer-than-normal glacial conditions through the summer are anticipated.

Active Monitoring Required

Madden–Julian Oscillation (MJO)

The MJO is a 30–60 day tropical wave of enhanced convection propagating eastward around the globe. When its active phase is positioned over the Indian Ocean during monsoon months, it can temporarily overwhelm El Niño suppression and deliver intense rainfall pulses across Pakistan.

🌀

MJO is the key wild-card for 2026. A well-timed MJO event over the Indian Ocean in July or August could dramatically alter the character of individual months within the season.
